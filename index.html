ANAPHYLAXIS

Anaphylaxis is a severe, rapid-onset allergic reaction that can be life-threatening. Early recognition and immediate treatment save lives.

Your mission: Complete the activities below to build your knowledge and confidence in recognizing and responding to anaphylaxis.

SCENARIO CHALLENGE

Read the scenario and choose the best actions in order.

Jamie, a 15-year-old student, eats a cookie containing peanuts. A few minutes later they start to feel itchy and their lips swell. They also complain of difficulty breathing.

What should you do? Put the steps in the correct order (1 = first step).

Lay them flat and elevate their legs.
Give epinephrine (if available).
Call emergency services immediately.
Stay with them and monitor their condition.
Remove them from the trigger (stop eating).
